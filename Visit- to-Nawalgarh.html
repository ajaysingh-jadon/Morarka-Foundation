Chairperson’s Visit to Nawalgarh: Support to Farmers and Women SHGs Strengthened

The Chairperson of Morarka Foundation, Smt. Bharti Morarka, along with Mrs. Pragati Mundhra, visited Nawalgarh and interacted with local farmers and Self-Help Group (SHG) women. Their gracious presence and active engagement made the two-day visit truly impactful and enriching for the entire community.

During the programme, spray machines were distributed to farmers to enhance agricultural efficiency. Additionally, financial assistance of ₹88 lakh was provided to 40 SHG women by Chairperson Ma'am & NABARD, enabling them to strengthen their livelihood activities and expand income-generating opportunities.

The Chairperson encouraged farmers to adopt organic and sustainable farming practices to improve soil health, reduce input costs, and enhance the quality of life for both land and community. Farmers were motivated to move toward chemical-free cultivation for long-term ecological and economic benefits.

The event also featured discussions on various government and NABARD-supported schemes aimed at empowering rural communities, promoting sustainable agriculture, and fostering women’s entrepreneurship.

However, the key highlight of the programme was the interaction between traditional farmers and new-age farmers, who are keen to carry forward their agricultural legacy while embracing modern practices. This exchange of experiences fostered learning, innovation, and motivation among the participants.

The programme concluded with an assurance of continued support and collaboration for the holistic development of farmers and SHG groups in the region.

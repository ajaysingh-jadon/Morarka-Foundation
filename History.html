Journey of Morarka Foundation

From humble beginnings to national recognition – milestones in sustainable development and organic agriculture.

Home / History

2024 | Persent

2024-Persent – Strengthening Market Connectivity

Expanded collaborations with domestic brands, exporters, and institutional buyers to ensure stable demand for farmer-grown organic produce.

2022 | 2024

2022-2024 – Integration of Technology for Certification & Traceability

Use of digital record-keeping, mobile-based ICS monitoring, geo-tagged inspections, and digital audit readiness solutions.

2020 | 2022

2020-2022 – Enhanced Focus on Sustainability & Climate Resilience

Projects incorporate water conservation, soil restoration, biodiversity enhancement, and climate-resilient agricultural systems

2018 | 2020

2018-2020 – Diversification into Niche Organic Crops

The Foundation supports growers in diversifying into quinoa, chia, pulses, spices, oilseeds, and high-value organic crops for premium domestic and export markets.

2016 | 2018

2016-2018 – Expansion of ICS/FPO

Support is extended to multiple FPOs for organic value chain development, governance training, and enterprise development.

2013 | 2016

2013-2016 – Digital Documentation & Monitoring Systems

Modernised documentation, GPS mapping of farms, and digital MIS systems are introduced to strengthen traceability and compliance.

2010 | 2013

2010-2013 – National Recognition for Organic Promotion

Government and development agencies recognise Morarka Foundation as one of the largest organic grower group facilitators in the country.

2007 | 2010

2007-2010 – Value Addition & Processing Initiatives

The Foundation ventures into processing, grading, and value-addition units to increase the economic return of organic produce.

2004 | 2007

2004-2007 – Community-Based Market Linkages

Introduction of structured procurement and market linkages for organic produce, enabling farmers to receive assured markets and better price realization.

2002 | 2004

2002-2004 – First Large-Scale Organic Certification Achieved

Morarka-supported grower groups secure their initial organic certifications under NPOP, establishing the Foundation as a national leader in group certification systems.

2000 | 2002

2000-2002 – Expansion to Multiple States

Organic farming projects expand across Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, and several other regions, connecting thousands of farmers to sustainable practices.

1997 | 2000

1997-2000 – Internal Control System

Launch of the Internal Control System (ICS) Model A structured Internal Control System is introduced for group certification, enabling small and marginal farmers to access organic certification efficiently.

1994 | 1997

1994-1997 – Introduction of Organic Farming Initiatives

The Foundation begins large-scale field programmes to promote organic agriculture, farmer training, and village-level capacity building.

1990 | 1994

1990-1994 – Establishment

Morarka Foundation is established with a vision to promote rural development, sustainable agriculture, and community-centric growth models across India.

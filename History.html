Journey of Morarka Foundation

From humble beginnings to national recognition – milestones in sustainable development and organic agriculture.

Home / Milestones

1990 | 1994

1991 – Establishment and Inception

Morarka Foundation was established in Nawalgarh, Rajasthan, with a clear mandate to promote rural development, sustainable agriculture, and community-driven growth models aimed at improving livelihoods in underserved communities.

1994 | 1998

1994–1998 – Introduction of Organic Farming Initiatives

The Foundation launched large-scale field interventions to promote organic farming through structured farmer training and village-level capacity building. These initiatives led to the successful cultivation and promotion of organic pulses and vegetables, laying the foundation for sustainable agricultural practices.

2000 | 2004

2002–2004 – Expansion into Research & Development

With a strong focus on innovation and appropriate technologies, Morarka Foundation significantly strengthened its research and development efforts. Its R&D journey, which began in 1995 with farm waste recycling using traditional and modern methods, emphasized the development and promotion of vermiculture technology. By 2000, the Foundation had emerged as India’s largest producer of vermicompost, reinforcing its leadership in organic inputs.

2004 | 2005

2004–2005 – Market Linkages & Retail Entry

Structured procurement and strong market linkages were established to ensure assured markets and better price realization for farmers. In 2005, Morarka Organic Food Limited (MOFL) was launched to directly connect farmers with consumers and institutional buyers, strengthening sustainable livelihoods.

2005 | 2006

2005 – ICS Launch & Organic Expansion

The Foundation introduced a structured Internal Control System (ICS) to enable cost-effective group organic certification, improving access for small and marginal farmers. Organic programmes expanded beyond Rajasthan to multiple states, with Morarka-supported groups securing their first NPOP certifications—marking the Foundation as a pioneer in group certification.

2006 | 2010

2006–2010 – Value Addition, Processing Initiatives & National Recognition

Morarka Foundation expanded into grading, processing, and value-addition infrastructure to strengthen the organic value chain and maximize farmer returns. During this period, the Foundation received national recognition from government agencies and development organizations as one of India’s largest facilitators of organic grower groups.

2013 | 2017

2013–2017 – Digitalization & Strengthening of ICS and FPOs

To enhance traceability and compliance, the Foundation introduced digital documentation systems, GPS-based farm mapping, and integrated MIS platforms. Concurrently, targeted support was extended to multiple Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) focusing on governance strengthening, organic value chain development, and enterprise creation.

2018 | 2019

2018–2019 – Diversification into Niche Organic Crops & Climate-Resilient Practices

Growers were supported in diversifying into high-value and niche organic crops such as quinoa, chia, pulses, spices, and oilseeds for premium domestic and export markets. During this phase, integrated climate-resilient agricultural practices—including water conservation, soil regeneration, biodiversity enhancement, and sustainable land-use models—were systematically implemented.

2020 | 2022

2020–2022 – COVID-19 Response, Farmer Support & Technology Integration

During the COVID-19 pandemic, the Foundation prioritized & safeguarded farmer livelihoods by ensuring uninterrupted market access and continuity of supply chains, resulting in excellent sales performance for organic produce. Simultaneously, advanced digital tools were deployed to strengthen certification and traceability systems, including mobile-based ICS monitoring, geo-tagged inspections, digital audit readiness, and enhanced traceability mechanisms.

2023 | Present

2023–Present – Strengthening Market Connectivity & Programme Expansion

The Foundation continues to deepen market linkages through collaborations with domestic brands, exporters, and institutional buyers, ensuring stable demand, fair pricing, and long-term market security for organic farmers. In parallel, Morarka Foundation is actively implementing and managing multiple organic farming projects across North India.

Value Chain Management

From farm to fork: Building sustainable and integrated organic food systems.

Food is produced, stored, and sold in isolated steps. This results in quality issues like contamination and unfair distribution of profits. Farmers often earn less than marketers and retailers.

Morarka Organic solved this by investing in the entire food value chain. From farm inputs to consumer products, they brought everything together under a unified system over 15 years.

By improving resource use, storage, packaging, and retail connections, they cut cultivation costs and reduced prices of organic food‚Äîbenefiting both producers and consumers.

Organic certification relies on process monitoring. This makes value chain management essential‚Äîsomething Morarka has mastered with decentralized solutions and grassroot entrepreneurs.

In India, food production remains disorganized. Morarka bridged this gap, developing infrastructure and technology for both fresh and processed organic products despite initial challenges.

Today, over 100,000 farmers on 250,000 acres grow organic crops, producing 300 retail products. Morarka has become a global model for sustainable and inclusive agricultural value chains.

Project Overview

Since 1995, Morarka Foundation has promoted microfinance in the Shekhawati region to strengthen rural women’s economic participation. Recognising women as key contributors to agriculture, livestock, and small enterprises, the Foundation positioned microfinance not as an end, but as a tool for social and economic empowerment.

Supported by NABARD, the programme has formed and trained Self-Help Groups (SHGs), linked them with banks, and enabled women to access credit for income-generating activities. To date, 1,115 SHGs with 11,490 women have been formed, resulting in over Rs. 355 crore in cumulative income and Rs. 41 crore in bank credit. The initiative continues to expand, with new SHGs and growing financial inclusion across rural communities.

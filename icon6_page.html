Sprinkler Irrigation Transformation

Facilitated irrigation access to over 10,000 farmers in Shekhawati region

Empowering Farmers Through Irrigation Innovation
  • Back in 1994–95, only about 10% of farmland in Shekhawati (Nawalgarh Block, Jhunjhunu) was under irrigation. Although the water table was shallow and the State Electricity Board provided connections, farmers were hesitant due to undulating terrain and sandy soils. The high cost of leveling—₹1 to ₹1.5 lakhs per acre—was a major deterrent.
  • After being awarded an Extension Services Project by the Department of Agriculture (GOR), Morarka Foundation introduced innovative technologies to over 10,000 farm families across 25 villages. Among the most impactful was the Sprinkler Irrigation System.
  • This system proved ideal for the region’s challenging conditions. It enabled effective irrigation on uneven, sandy land and delivered yields comparable to leveled fields—without the high cost of land leveling.
  • The success led to a surge in demand for electrical connections, with wait times of up to 4–5 years. Still, farmers saw this as a gateway to prosperity.
  • As irrigation improved, farmers began relocating to their fields, planting trees, and cultivating vegetables—initially for self-use, later for income. Many adopted integrated farming by rearing dairy animals, turning milk into ghee for commercial sale.
  • In just a decade, irrigated land expanded to over 40%, with 100% of it using sprinkler systems. This marks a remarkable transformation driven by innovation, community engagement, and sustained effort.

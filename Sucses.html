A. E Library

Morarka Foundation has established its 22nd E-Library in Nawalgarh, offering high-speed internet, Wi-Fi–enabled computer labs, and a rich digital learning environment. Built on a client–server model, the center provides access to e-books, magazines, documentaries, and online education aligned with academic syllabi.

The E-Library bridges the digital gap in rural areas, enabling students, women, and senior citizens to learn essential computer skills, English communication, fast mathematics, science and technology, personality development, and more. Special educational videos for children with developmental challenges are also available.

Over the past two years, the E-Library has hosted summer camps, welcomed hundreds of school visits, and empowered learners—including rural, differently-abled, and economically disadvantaged groups—with accessible, high-quality digital education.

Today, Nawalgarh’s E-Library stands as a vibrant hub for skill development, creativity, and lifelong learning—bringing the possibilities of the digital world within everyone’s reach.

  • Morarka Foundation has started E-Library facility to make the easy Internet access to various stake holders at Kamal Morarka Town Hall, Nawalgarh.
  • Students have been availing the online education facility at the center to solve queries regarding their subjects.
  • The E-Library center has become a very important sources of skill development, computer learning programs have been running for students.
  • A video base basic education program is also running through E-Library Center for mental disability children.
  • Rural women also approach at the center to develop their skill through i.e. computer courses, attend online cooking classes, dance classes, e-commerce etc.

B. Bringing Modern Education to Rural Indiar

India is advancing rapidly in science, technology, and innovation—but these opportunities often reach only urban, privileged students. To bridge this divide, Morarka Foundation has introduced modern online education to rural regions, ensuring that village students can access the same high-quality learning available in major cities.

This initiative opens new pathways for rural youth, strengthens their technical skills, and encourages them to pursue careers in engineering and other modern fields. Morarka Foundation remains committed to empowering rural India with knowledge, opportunity, and confidence to participate in the country’s growth story.

In partnership with a Mumbai-based institution, the Foundation launched an online engineering preparation program in Berri Village, Sikar. Students can now learn advanced subjects at school or from home through digital modules, without the need for expensive coaching or migration to cities.
